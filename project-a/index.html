[ CHITSUNE ]

Scientific Name:

Vulpes lagopus

Discovered by / at:

Christina Chueng, 2025

Family:

When they are young they stay with their mothers, but when they reach of age, they leave to be on their own.

Habitat:

The wilderness away from human civilization.

Appearance:

They are mostly white, except for some red spots on the their ears, eyes, and tails. When young they only have one tail, but with age they get more.

Key Characteristics:

  • Independant.
  • Sly and playful.
  • Disobedient.
  • Enjoys sleeping.

What we know so far about the chitsune? The chitsune is a fox like creature that belongs to the fox family. The ones with more tails signify older age. While they sometimes come in different colors, the most common is white with spots of red. Not much is known about the chitsune's family, as researchers have been unable to make contact long enough to document the interactions. It is speculated that the young ones typically stay with their parents until at least growing their second tail, as a chitsune with only one tail has never been spotted. Most chitsune spottings, they are typically alone and independant creatures, often seen without other chitsune. Traditionally, chitsune have loved to play tricks on humans, making them confused and shocked. It is best known to never be too careful as even your closest friend could secretly be a chitsune. Although this is a popular story told amongst children to never be too trusting of outsiders.
Guidelines for human intraction with this creature If seen in the wild, do NOT approach. You have been warned...
